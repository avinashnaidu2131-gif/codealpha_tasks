# PHISHING AWARENESS TRAINING

## Slide 1 — Title
Phishing Awareness Training  
Protecting Yourself from Online Scams

---

## Slide 2 — What is Phishing?
Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick people into sharing:
- Passwords
- Bank details
- OTPs
- Personal information

They pretend to be trusted organizations like:
- Banks
- Companies
- Government offices
- Social media platforms

---

## Slide 3 — Types of Phishing
- Email Phishing – Fake emails
- SMS Phishing (Smishing) – Fake text messages
- Voice Phishing (Vishing) – Scam phone calls
- Website Phishing – Fake login pages

---

## Slide 4 — How to Recognize Phishing Emails
Red Flags:
- Urgent language (e.g. “Your account will be locked”)
- Unknown sender address
- Spelling and grammar mistakes
- Suspicious links
- Attachments you didn’t expect

---

## Slide 5 — How to Recognize Fake Websites
Check:
- URL spelling (example: amaz0n.com instead of amazon.com)
- HTTPS lock symbol
- Strange pop-ups
- Login pages asking for too much information

---

## Slide 6 — Social Engineering Tactics
Attackers use:
- Fear – “Your account is hacked”
- Trust – “I’m from IT support”
- Rewards – “You won a prize”
- Urgency – “Act now”

---

## Slide 7 — Best Practices
- Never click unknown links
- Check sender addresses carefully
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Use strong passwords
- Keep software updated
- Report suspicious messages

---

## Slide 8 — Real World Example
Example:
A user receives an email claiming to be from a bank asking them to “verify account details” using a link.
The link leads to a fake website that steals login information.

---

## Slide 9 — What To Do If You Fall for Phishing
- Change passwords immediately
- Inform your bank or organization
- Scan your device for malware
- Report the phishing attempt

---

## Slide 10 — Summary
Phishing is preventable if you:
- Stay alert
- Verify before clicking
- Think before sharing
